Giant pandas resemble bears with a head and body length of 1.2-1.8 meters and tail length of 10-12 cm. Weight 80-120 kg. Generally, males are slightly larger than females. The coat color is black and white. The panda belongs to the preparation of many males. They have a small number of litters in their lives, and the cubs are not easy to survive, which makes the panda population grow very slowly.

Macroscopic features:

Wild giant pandas live in high mountains and deep valleys in the upper reaches of the Yangtze River. The climate is cool and humid, and their humidity is often above 80%.

Dietary habits:

It almost entirely depends on eating bamboo for its livelihood, accounting for 99% of the annual food, among which the most favorite foods are the arrow bamboo and the Chinese arrow bamboo.

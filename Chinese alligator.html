Living environmental and Reproduction：

Chinese alligators like to inhabit lakes, swampy beaches, or hills and mountain streams in moist areas full of chaotic grass. Chinese alligators are eggs, and in early June, alligators mate in the water. Towards the beginning of July, female alligators began to build circular nests in suitable places for laying eggs, about 10-30 eggs per nest.

Macroscopic features：

The Chinese alligator is 1-2m long, has a flat head, a prominent snout, and stubby limbs. The tail is long and flat, stout and powerful. Their heads are relatively large. The weight is about 36 kg. The scales have granular and banded lines.

Dietary habits：

It likes to eat: wild ducks, hares, frogs, mice, fish, snails and other animals.

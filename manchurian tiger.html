Living environmental and Reproduction：

he Siberian tiger is a typical mountain forest-dwelling animal, in the deciduous broad-leaved forest and coniferous broad-leaved mixed forest in the north. In winter estrus mating, the estrus mating period is generally from November to February of the following year, every 2-4 litters, the lactation period is 5-6 months, and the female tiger can only breed once every 2-3 years.

Macroscopic features：

An adult male Siberian tiger weighs an average of 250 kg and has a head and body length of about 2.3 m. The whole body is covered with black stripes, and the black horizontal stripes on the forehead are slightly colluding.

Dietary habits:

Prey on wild boar, and ungulates such as red deer, sambar, roe deer, musk deer, deer, and deer, occasionally prey on wild fowl, and also eat berries and large insects in autumn.
